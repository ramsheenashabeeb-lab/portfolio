Sub:Energy harvesting by eco friendly piezoelectric materials

Energy harvesting using eco-friendly piezoelectric materials converts mechanical energy from ambient sources like vibrations into electrical energy. This process uses the piezoelectric effect, where a material generates an electric charge in response to mechanical stress. Eco-friendly materials are developed as alternatives to traditional, lead-based piezoelectric ceramics to address environmental concern

Each and every organism alive and society as a whole relies heavily on energy as a form of technology development. The increased availability of electronic devices resulted in a proportional increase in energy demand. As a result, there has been a heightened emphasis on the adoption of renewable energy sources. Energy harvesting is one such renewable source that shows potential. It refers to a process that captures energy drawn from ambient sources such as movement, heat, light, and vibrations, and stores it for later use. Out of many methods of energy harvesting, piezoelectric energy harvesting is noteworthy. This method focuses on using piezoelectric materials and in turn gives a reason for the high demand of these materials due to their capability to change mechanical energy into electrical energy

The joining of electric charge and mechanical stress is referred to as the piezoelectric effect, which is both a stress induced phenomenon and a property of certain materials. Lead zirconate titanate, also known as PZT, is widely accepted for use as a piezoelectric material due to its exceptional piezoelectric properties. Unfortunately, some materials which are conventional piezoelectric come along with some poisonous elements like lead which makes them extremely unfriendly to nature. In light of recent developments and concerns regarding the environment along with strict orders on known deadly substances, there is an urgent shift in focus required towards developing eco-friendly piezoelectric materials that are convenient, doable, and efficient in the harvesting of energy.
